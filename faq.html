BYOM & Model Architecture

BYOM means you bring your own API keys for commercial model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Mistral, etc.) and connect them to the Drayvn platform. Drayvn never proxies through its own model contracts — you maintain your direct relationship with every model provider, including pricing, terms, and data processing agreements.

In practice: You add your OpenAI API key to Drayvn's settings. All calls to GPT-4 go directly from Drayvn's orchestration layer to OpenAI using your key. Drayvn charges you a transparent platform markup (10% on Starter, 5% on Growth, 0% on Enterprise) for the governance, orchestration, and routing layer — not for the compute itself.

You can also host open-source models (Llama-3, Mistral, Phi-3) entirely on your Dell Pro Max hardware, in which case there's literally $0 per-token cost on that model forever.

Security, Privacy & Compliance

Physical isolation means the hardware running your AI inference is a machine you own, in a building you control. "Logical isolation" — what cloud providers offer — means a software configuration on their hardware says your data is separate from other customers. Both have strong legal agreements. Only physical isolation offers a technical guarantee.

Why does it matter? Three scenarios:

  • Healthcare (HIPAA): Patient data physically never leaving the hospital network eliminates an entire category of compliance risk that legal agreements alone can't address.
  • Legal: Client-privileged information processed on your law firm's own machine has no chain-of-custody question for opposing counsel to exploit.
  • Nation-state threat models: For defense contractors or critical infrastructure, "our AI server is in our classified facility" is a statement you can make with a Dell Pro Max that you simply cannot make with any cloud service.

All prompts and inference results are logged in your own Langfuse observability instance — either self-hosted on your infrastructure or in a dedicated cloud tenant accessible only by your team.

Drayvn's control plane (the orchestration layer in GCP) routes requests and manages agent state but does not store prompt content. For Enterprise Hybrid customers, even the routing metadata is minimized — the edge hardware handles inference locally and only sends coordination signals to the control plane.

Drayvn staff access to customer environments requires customer-initiated support tickets and is logged with full audit trail. There is no standing access to customer data or prompts.

The Sentinel runs on your local Dell Pro Max (or as a cloud-side module on Starter/Growth) and scans every prompt and response using a combination of:

  • Pattern matching: SSNs, credit card numbers, phone numbers, email addresses, IP addresses, passport formats, and 50+ common PII patterns recognized out of the box
  • Named entity recognition (NER): ML-based detection of names, organizations, locations, and medical terms in context
  • Custom patterns: Enterprise customers can define their own regex or ML patterns for proprietary identifiers (employee IDs, client codes, internal project names)

Detected PII is replaced with a labeled token: "John Smith (SSN: 234-56-7890)" becomes "[PERSON: JS-4421] (SSN: [REDACTED-XXX-XX-7890])". The original → token mapping is stored in an encrypted vault on your infrastructure, never in Drayvn's systems.

Platform, Model Studio & Pricing

Model Studio is available now on Enterprise Hybrid plans. It works in two output modes:

Custom RAG Knowledge Base: Upload documents (PDFs, Word, spreadsheets, web links) and connect data sources (databases, SharePoint, Google Drive, APIs). Drayvn chunks, embeds, and indexes everything into a private vector store on your Dell Pro Max. Your Enterprise Copilot and agents query this index to answer questions grounded in your specific enterprise data.

Fine-Tuned Custom Model: Using the same uploaded data, Drayvn generates synthetic Q&A training pairs using an LLM, then fine-tunes a base model (Llama-3, Mistral, Phi-3) overnight on your Dell Pro Max hardware. The resulting model has internalized your domain knowledge — it's not retrieval, it's built-in expertise. Training runs entirely on your hardware. No cloud vendor sees your training data.

The typical pipeline is: upload documents → review auto-generated Q&A pairs (optional curation step) → kick off fine-tuning → model available in your BYOM routing within 12–24 hours.
